The year as a civic ladder

52 Weeks

The early weeks ask how ordinary people can understand public life. Later weeks follow money, work, housing, transport, health, justice and resilience.

The route through the year

The 52 weeks are not arranged by moral importance. They are arranged as a ladder: first make public life readable, then answerable, then fairer.

  1. Weeks 1–8: Readability, ownership, questions, consultations, information rights, procurement, AI and digital access.
  2. Weeks 9–16: Public money, poverty pay, fair contracts, secure hours, enforcement and progression.
  3. Weeks 17–24: Workplace dignity, occupational health, return-to-work safety, NHS administration and patient information.
  4. Weeks 25–32: Household survival: hardship schemes, advice, deductions, debt, essentials, fuel poverty and resilience.
  5. Weeks 33–40: Housing: registers, temporary accommodation, renting, rent subsidy, empty homes and supported housing.
  6. Weeks 41–48: Transport, non-driver rights, skills, retraining, local productivity, high streets and care.
  7. Weeks 49–52: Youth exclusion, prison release, probation resettlement and climate adaptation for ordinary homes.

The tracker shows six entries at a time. Each week appears as a field card so the year can be explored without becoming a spreadsheet.
