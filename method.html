Method

The method is deliberately simple: one issue, one workflow check, one evidence base, one reasonable ask and one public log.

The weekly process

  1. Pick one issue.
  2. Define the problem in plain English.
  3. Check whether a route or workflow already exists.
  4. Gather evidence from public sources.
  5. Make one reasonable ask.
  6. Contact the right people through the right route.
  7. Log what happened.
  8. Accept reasonable compromise or reconfigure if needed.

First rule

Do not invent a new campaign before checking whether the thing already exists.

Conduct rules

  • Criticise systems, decisions and policies before individuals.
  • Give people a fair chance to respond.
  • Do not publish private contact details.
  • Do not encourage dogpiling.
  • Separate fact, inference and opinion.
  • Correct errors publicly.
  • Use formal routes before escalation.
  • Keep the tone civil even when the issue is serious.
